Overview of Star Topology

Star topology is a network topology where each device in the network is connected to a central point, which can be a hub, switch, or router. This central point acts as a distribution point for data transmission. Unlike bus or ring topologies where devices are connected in a linear or circular fashion, star topology resembles a centralized layout with the central point at its core.


Diagram of Star Topology

Hub or Switch

The central component of a star network is typically a hub or a switch. This device manages and controls the flow of data between connected devices. Hubs operate at the physical layer (Layer 1) of the OSI model, while switches operate at the data link layer (Layer 2) and are more efficient in handling traffic.

End Devices

These are the peripheral devices (such as computers, printers, servers, etc.) that connect to the central hub or switch via individual network cables. Each device has its own dedicated connection to the central point.
